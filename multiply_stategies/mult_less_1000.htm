(**) Multiplying Two Numbers Less Than 1000, But Close To 1000:

   A.  This method is very similar to section XII and comes from the algebra:

(1000 – a) (1000 – b) = 1000((1000 – a) – b) + ab

   B.  Using numbers instead of variables we get the following:

1.  Find the difference between both the numbers and 1000.

2.  Multiply these two values together and write it down.  Make sure the answer takes up 3 place values.

3.  Subtract the difference found in step 1 of one of the numbers with the remaining number.  Write the result.

Ex [1]  994 x 989 =_________.

a)      1000 – 994 = 6.

b)      1000 – 989 = 11.

c)      6 x 11 = 66.  Write 066 to take up 3 place values.

d)      994 – 11 = 983.  Write 983.  (You can also use 989 – 6 = 983)

e)      The answer is 983066.

Ex [2]  9882 =_________.

a)      1000 – 988 = 12.

b)      12 x 12 = 144.  Write 144.

c)      988 – 12 = 976.  Write 976.

d)      The answer is 976144.
