Working With Polynomials:

A.  On number sense tests you have to deal with two different types of polynomials.

1.  Quadratic Equation:

a.  A quadratic equation is an equation in this form:

ax2 + bx + c = 0.

b.  The sum of the roots is: -b/a

c.  The product of the roots is: c/a

d.  The discriminant is: b2 - 4ac

1.  If the discriminant is positive, there are 2 real roots.

2.  If the discriminant is negative, there are 0 real roots.

3.  If the discriminant is zero, there is only 1 real root.

2.  Polynomial of degree 3:

a.  A polynomial of degree 3 is in this form:

ax3 + bx2 + cx + d = 0.

b.  The sum of the roots is: -b/a

c.  The product of the roots is: -d/a

d.  The sum of the product of the roots is: c/a (sometimes it might say taken two at a time)

B.  Here are some examples how the above information is used.

Ex [1]  The sum of the roots of 3x2 + 6x - 2 = 0 is __________.

a.  The sum of the roots is -b/a.

b.  The answer is -6/3 or -2.

Ex [2]  The product of the roots of x3 - 6x2 + 3x - 8 = 0 is ______.

a.  The product of the roots is -d/a.

b.  The answer is -(-8)/1 or 8.

Ex [3]  How many real roots does 2x2 - 3x + 4 = 0 have? ______.

a.  We substitute using the formula for the discriminant.

b.  (-3)2 - 4(2)(4) = 9 - 32 = -23.

c.  Since the number is negative, the equation has 0 real roots.

d.  The answer is 0.
